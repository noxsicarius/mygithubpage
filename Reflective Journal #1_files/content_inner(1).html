Research Article Discussion Part 1
(Part 2 is below this)
When I was young, my family and I always made it a point to keep the area we used outdoors cleaner than when we arrived. This is something I continue to do as I personally hate being in an area with garbage all over the place. I never really felt as if it were a chore, it was just something that seemed like it should be done. I continue to do this today as I hate being in an area with large amounts of garbage and I found that if you clean an area relatively well, less people will throw garbage around because they feel worse when they are the one starting the problem rather than contributing to it. Interestingly enough the amount of community service people participate in also depends on the state they live in. When I lived in California, to graduate the 5
th
 grade you had to attend a field trip with the other classes to clean up a wetlands area or have a valid excuse to be exempt. You were not allowed to pass until you had proof of community service such as if you attended the trip. My friends and I actually made a game out of it to see who could clean up the most garbage, but a large number of the students seemed miserable. I later moved here, where community service seemed to be less emphasized. There was no talk at all about community service during middle school, but I would help my neighborhood set up the public events they did such as a fish fry or kids play day. Continuing on after this, I was one of the few in my entire high school that did any community service at all and one of the even fewer who wanted to and enjoyed it. I did some work around my neighborhood still, but also volunteered to do robotics demos, worked to organize and distribute donated clothing to those in need, and even taught Lego Robotics to elementary school students. That is my background before college, and quite frankly this is not much different than high school. I know some people who have done community service, but only a few did it because they want to help people or want to make things better somehow. Most of the people I hear about doing community service only do it because they need it for school or they believe it will help them get a job. This was discussed in the reading and from personal experience I can tell everyone that it is true. Even when you
can see the joy and satisfaction it brings others, some of the participants still seem like they don’t want to
be there. People just seems so focused on what makes them happy or what benefits them rather than
what benefits others or even what benefits the world. There seems to be a “What does it do for me?”
mentality in the general populous. One thing I would really like to explain to anyone who may find the time to read this is that community service is not just what you physically go out and do such as cleaning up a park. It is understandable that as college students you may not have time to go out and do these things, but there are other ways to help your community that most people never consider. This can include things you do online for people; maybe you post tutorials for free that can really help people, maybe you upload your notes from class to the disability services department on campus, or maybe you just release some computer software you developed for personal use that may help others (this only applies to software YOU made, releasing other software is illegal). If it helps a community of people, then it is community service and is definitely worth doing.

Considering both of our most positive community service experiences seem to have stemmed from helping children (even though I was still one myself at the time) it brings the thought to mind that maybe if people are exposed to community service that helps children rather than something like cleaning up the environment then maybe more people our age would be less annoyed at the thought of performing community service and possibly be more active in it.
